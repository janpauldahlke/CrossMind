##  **Team formation**

You can participate in teams of 2–4 people. The sweet spot is usually 2–3: enough range to cover product, build, and pitch, but small enough to move fast without heavy coordination.

The best move is to find teammates before the event. That is a fair advantage: you save the morning search and alignment time, arrive with roles mostly clear, and start building as soon as the event begins.

Use Discord before June 6 to introduce yourself: say what you can build or contribute, what customer or workflow you understand, and what kind of teammate you are looking for.

Solo builders are allowed, but we strongly recommend finding at least one teammate. A one-person team can work if the scope is extremely narrow, but it is much harder to build, validate, design, and pitch well in one day. If you arrive solo, use Discord \#find-your-team and the morning window to find one or two strong teammates.

###  **What makes a good team**

* One person owns build / engineering speed.

* One person owns product / customer clarity.

* One person owns pitch, demo flow, and evidence.

* Everyone can explain the problem and help with the final pitch.

Keep the team small and decisive. Avoid spending the morning debating the perfect idea. Pick a painful problem, define a narrow demo, assign owners, and start building.

###  **Good team-formation questions**

* What can each person ship today?

* Who understands the customer or workflow best?

* Who can make the demo believable by 19:00?

* Who will keep the pitch and submission on track?

* Can we explain the idea in one sentence before we start building?

##  **Pitch deck: max 7 slides**

Every slide must do work.

1. **Problem \+ customer** \- specific pain, who has it (role, company size, industry), and what they do today to solve it, even badly.

2. **Solution \+ product** \- what you built, how it works, demo link or QR code if available.

3. **Why now** \- what changed that makes this possible or urgent now: a new API, a regulation, a cost collapse. For AI startups: what makes this more than a wrapper?

4. **Market \+ competition** \- bottom-up sizing (customer count × revenue per customer, not "1% of a $100B market"). Direct competitors, indirect alternatives, status quo, and why you win. Never say "we have no competitors."

5. **Business model \+ traction / evidence** \- who pays, how much, how often, and what you validated.

6. **Go-to-market** \- how the first 50 customers find you. Name a channel you can actually run, not "we'll do SEO" or "we'll go viral."

7. **Team** \- why this team has the right edge. End with contact info.

For inspiration: [50 real startup pitch decks](https://www.figma.com/design/9luacSyyLrJs3BKJOUlOBU/50-startup-pitch-decks?node-id=0-1&t=2WMi3DVWy8CCqlRR-1) \- see how the best decks are built.

##  **How the day works**

##  **How judges decide**

The full judging and scoring guide is here: [https://docs.google.com/document/d/1oYZ8MwMlcYTdA2wAQIUTBexxxzLDJrz9i4SZZdjNZnA](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1oYZ8MwMlcYTdA2wAQIUTBexxxzLDJrz9i4SZZdjNZnA)

The spirit is simple: could this become a real company, and did the team execute under pressure? 

Judges will look for:

* A painful, specific buyer problem.

* A working demo or credible hosted preview, if submitted.

* A sharp wedge, not a broad platform claim.

* Clear use of AI or modern tooling where it actually improves the product.

* Evidence that someone wants this: user signal, customer conversation, waitlist, pilot interest, usage, or credible domain insight.

* A team that learns quickly and can explain why they are the right people to build it.

You do not need to build the next OpenAI in 12 hours. You need to ship a working product, a clear pitch, and a reason this could only have been built by you.

To understand exactly how judges think \- and how to sharpen your idea, wedge, and pitch to meet that bar \- read the [Ideas & Strategy guide](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1K3fbTFPysRgACCj0FzxrL0VB2-ebbUDw_tRNqpYJ7YQ) before June 6\. It is the single most useful thing you can do to prepare.
